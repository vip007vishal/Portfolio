WHY THIS PROJECT?

Empowering Farmers with Intelligent Technology

Traditional farming methods often depend on manual inspection, making it difficult to accurately monitor changing environmental conditions. Excessive irrigation can waste valuable water, while insufficient irrigation can negatively impact crop growth.

JeevanDhara365 bridges this gap by combining IoT devices, environmental sensing, cloud-based monitoring, and intelligent automation into one comprehensive farming ecosystem. Farmers can continuously monitor field conditions, make informed decisions, and improve productivity with minimal manual effort.

The platform is designed with scalability in mind, making it suitable for home gardens, greenhouses, educational research, and large-scale agricultural applications.

HARDWARE COMPONENTS

Devices Used in the Project

ESP32 Development Board

Central processing unit of the project that reads sensor values, processes information and communicates with cloud platforms through Wi-Fi.

DHT11 / DHT22

Measures environmental temperature and humidity for monitoring climate conditions.

Capacitive Soil Moisture Sensor

Determines the moisture content present in soil to support smart irrigation decisions.

Rain Sensor

Detects rainfall and helps avoid unnecessary irrigation during rainy conditions.

Ultrasonic Sensor

Measures water level or distance depending upon the application requirements.

PIR Motion Sensor

Detects movement around the agricultural field for monitoring and security purposes.

Sound Sensor

Measures surrounding sound intensity that can be used for environmental observation.

RC522 RFID Module

Supports RFID-based identification for authorized access and future smart farming applications.

HX711 Load Cell Module

Measures weight through load cells and enables future crop or storage monitoring applications.
